# SolidWorks Bicycle — Custom Suspension Design

MEng Mechanical Engineering · University of Sussex
Software: SolidWorks 2024 · SolidWorks Motion

---

Full bicycle assembly with a custom-engineered rear suspension system, modelled from scratch in SolidWorks. Every component — frame, swing arm, shock, drivetrain mounts, and wheel interfaces — was drawn as an individual part and assembled with full mate constraints. The suspension geometry was designed analytically before modelling, with kinematics verified in SolidWorks Motion. Documented in a 116-page technical design report.

![Assembly Preview](preview.png)

## Overview

The suspension system was designed from first principles — geometry selected to optimise ride characteristics, with kinematic analysis covering travel, linkage ratios, and anti-squat behaviour. Component sizing was justified through structural calculations and compared against design targets before finalising the model.
